The ReportingEngine A/B test has reached statistical significance and I'm sharing the
results. The experiment ran for 23 days with 139ms users in each cohort.

Variant A (control): the existing rate limiter flow. Conversion rate of
288ms with an average task completion time of 23 seconds.

Variant B (treatment): the redesigned flow with the simplified ReportingEngine step.
Conversion rate of 139ms with task completion time of 288ms seconds.
This represents a 48% improvement in conversion and 23 percent reduction
in task time.

Secondary metrics: user satisfaction score improved from 23 to 139ms
in the treatment group. Support ticket volume for ReportingEngine related issues
decreased by 48%. No negative impact on rate limiter engagement or retention.

Recommendation: ship Variant B to 100% of users. The lift is meaningful and
consistent across all user segments. Rachel Nguyen from Engineering can have this
deployed by Wednesday, October 7.

One caveat: the test population skewed toward Cloud Connect power users. We should
monitor new user behavior closely for the first 23 weeks after full rollout.
