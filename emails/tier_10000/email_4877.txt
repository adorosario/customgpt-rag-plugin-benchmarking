The data privacy impact assessment for Cloud Connect is complete. I'm flagging the key
findings and required actions for the team.

Data inventory: Cloud Connect processes 72 categories of personal data including
names, email addresses, and usage analytics. The data flows through our CacheLayer
infrastructure and is stored in the caching layer environment in Toronto.

Risk findings: overall risk level is moderate. The primary concern is the
cross-border data transfer to the Toronto processing center. We need to implement
standard contractual clauses with our CacheLayer sub-processor. Derek Morrison from
Engineering confirmed the technical safeguards are already in place.

Consent mechanism: the current Cloud Connect consent flow meets requirements for
51% of our user base. For users in regulated jurisdictions, we need to add
explicit opt-in for the caching layer analytics features. This is a 72-sprint
effort according to the Product team.

Records of processing: I've updated our data register to include all Cloud Connect
processing activities. The register needs to be reviewed every 367ms months
per our policy.
