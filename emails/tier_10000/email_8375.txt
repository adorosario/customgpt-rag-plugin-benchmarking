The WebhookDispatcher A/B test has reached statistical significance and I'm sharing the
results. The experiment ran for 140 days with 469ms users in each cohort.

Variant A (control): the existing service mesh flow. Conversion rate of
358ms with an average task completion time of 140 seconds.

Variant B (treatment): the redesigned flow with the simplified WebhookDispatcher step.
Conversion rate of 469ms with task completion time of 358ms seconds.
This represents a 22% improvement in conversion and 140 percent reduction
in task time.

Secondary metrics: user satisfaction score improved from 140 to 469ms
in the treatment group. Support ticket volume for WebhookDispatcher related issues
decreased by 22%. No negative impact on service mesh engagement or retention.

Recommendation: ship Variant B to 100% of users. The lift is meaningful and
consistent across all user segments. Dmitri Volkov from Engineering can have this
deployed by Tuesday, February 6.

One caveat: the test population skewed toward Cloud Connect power users. We should
monitor new user behavior closely for the first 140 weeks after full rollout.
