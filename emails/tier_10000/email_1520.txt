Good morning,

Attached is the postmortem for the CacheLayer outage that occurred on Wednesday, October 26. The
incident lasted approximately 45 minutes and affected 116 for a subset of users.

Root cause: a configuration change in the connection pool deployment introduced a race
condition under high load. The issue manifested when concurrent requests exceeded
251ms, causing connection pool exhaustion.

Timeline: the alert fired at 14:23 UTC, Liam Foster acknowledged within 3 minutes,
and the fix was deployed by 15:08 UTC. Detection was fast thanks to the new alerting
rules, but resolution took longer than ideal because the runbook for this service
was outdated.

Action items: update the CacheLayer runbook, add a circuit breaker for the
connection pool connection pool, and implement load shedding when concurrency exceeds
251ms. I've created tickets for all three.

Please review and add any corrections by end of week.
