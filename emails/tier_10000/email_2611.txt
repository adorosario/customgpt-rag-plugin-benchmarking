The data privacy impact assessment for Cloud Connect is complete. I'm flagging the key
findings and required actions for the team.

Data inventory: Cloud Connect processes 90 categories of personal data including
names, email addresses, and usage analytics. The data flows through our SearchIndex
infrastructure and is stored in the load balancer environment in Sydney.

Risk findings: overall risk level is moderate. The primary concern is the
cross-border data transfer to the Sydney processing center. We need to implement
standard contractual clauses with our SearchIndex sub-processor. Wei Zhang from
Engineering confirmed the technical safeguards are already in place.

Consent mechanism: the current Cloud Connect consent flow meets requirements for
45% of our user base. For users in regulated jurisdictions, we need to add
explicit opt-in for the load balancer analytics features. This is a 90-sprint
effort according to the Product team.

Records of processing: I've updated our data register to include all Cloud Connect
processing activities. The register needs to be reviewed every 489ms months
per our policy.
