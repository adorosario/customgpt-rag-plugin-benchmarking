Sharing the load testing results for WebhookDispatcher v2.6.0 ahead of next week's
release. We tested at 2x and 5x current peak traffic levels.

At 2x load: all endpoints stayed within SLA. P99 latency for the queue processor
endpoint was 345ms, well under our 200ms target. No errors
observed across the full test duration.

At 5x load: the WebhookDispatcher service held up but the queue processor started showing
degradation at around 3.5x. Error rate climbed to 21% and response times
increased significantly. The bottleneck appears to be the database connection pool
â€” we max out at the configured limit.

Recommendation: we're safe for the near-term growth projections. For the 156
milestone we're targeting in Q4, we'll need to increase the connection pool size
and potentially add a read replica. Aisha Okafor is putting together a capacity
planning proposal.
